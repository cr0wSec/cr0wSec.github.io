This is the first post in an ongoing series of field notes and research write-ups on offensive and defensive security.

## What to expect

Over time I plan to publish on:

- **Offensive security** — exploitation, red teaming, and attacker tradecraft
- **Detection engineering** — building, testing, and tuning detections
- **Threat intelligence** — actor and campaign analysis
- **OSINT** — collection, verification, and tradecraft
- **Cryptography** — protocol deep-dives and implementation pitfalls
- **Post-quantum** — the long road to PQ migration

## Ground rules

Posts here are practical, opinionated, and occasionally wrong. The goal is to share things I wish I had found when I was first working through a problem, not to ship polished white papers.

Corrections, pull requests, and pointers to prior art are welcome.
